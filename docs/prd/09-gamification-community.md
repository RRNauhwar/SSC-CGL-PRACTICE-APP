# 09 — Gamification & Community

Motivation and accountability engines. Gamification exists to drive **consistency** (the #1 predictor of SSC success); community drives **accountability + peer learning**. Both are carefully bounded so they never distract from actual score improvement — every mechanic rewards *learning behavior*, not vanity.

---

## Part A — Gamification

### A.1 Core currencies & progression

- **XP** — earned for meaningful learning actions (correct-after-struggle, completing SRS due items, finishing a mock + review, mastering a concept). XP is **weighted by difficulty and weightage**, so grinding easy questions yields little; fixing high-weightage weaknesses yields a lot. This aligns fun with score impact.
- **Levels** — XP thresholds; cosmetic + unlocks (themes, profile flair). Never gate learning content behind levels.
- **Coins** — soft currency from quests/streaks; spend on cosmetics, streak-freezes, extra AI explanations (within fair-use), or donate to scholarship pool.
- **Streaks** — daily "meaningful study" streak (must include real practice/revision, not just app-open). **Streak-freeze** items prevent guilt spirals; missed-day recovery (doc 08) protects motivation.

### A.2 Achievements, badges, trophies, certificates

- **Badges/achievements:** milestones tied to learning ("Cleared 500 weak-concept reviews", "3 mocks with <2 careless mistakes", "Mastered all Time & Work concepts").
- **Learning trophies** for mastery milestones per subject.
- **Completion certificates** (shareable) for finishing a plan, a subject's syllabus, or a mock series — social proof + motivation.

### A.3 Quests & missions

- **Daily quests** (3 small, personalized, high-utility tasks from the recommender, doc 06).
- **Weekly / monthly missions** (e.g., "Improve Reasoning accuracy by 5%", "Complete 4 sectional mocks").
- **Season Pass** (P1): time-boxed seasons with a free + premium track of rewards; goals are always study-behavior-based.
- **Rank progression** (tiers like Bronze→...→Grandmaster) based on a learning-quality score, not just volume.

### A.4 Social gamification

- **Leaderboards:** global, friends, and cohort/institute — ranked by **learning-quality score** (mastery gain + consistency + mock performance), with anti-gaming safeguards; opt-in and privacy-respecting.
- **Challenge friends:** head-to-head on a topic/mock set.

### A.5 Guardrails (so gamification helps, not harms)

- Reward *quality* (mastery, accuracy, retention), not raw time/volume → prevents burnout-farming.
- No dark patterns: no loss-framing that induces anxiety; streak protection built in; easy to mute competitive elements.
- Leaderboards are opt-in; a "focus mode" hides all gamification for users who prefer it.

### A.6 Metrics
- Streak length distribution, quest completion, D7/D30 retention lift from gamification (A/B), correlation of learning-quality score with real score improvement.

## Part B — Community

### B.1 Features

- **Discussion forums** — per exam/subject/chapter; ask doubts, share strategy.
- **Question comments & solution sharing** — threaded discussion on any PYQ; users post alternative solutions/tricks (peer-reviewed, upvoted; best solutions surface and can be promoted into the question's alt-solutions after moderation).
- **Study groups** — small accountability groups with shared goals, group streaks, and a shared plan view.
- **Mentorship** — verified toppers/mentors answer doubts; opt-in mentor matching (P2).
- **Friends / followers** — lightweight social graph for challenges + leaderboards.
- **Competitive challenges & mock battles** — real-time or async head-to-head on a test; post-battle comparison analytics.
- **Peer comparison** — see how your accuracy/speed on a chapter compares to similar aspirants (anonymized cohort).

### B.2 Trust, safety & moderation

- **Moderation pipeline:** automated toxicity/spam filtering + user reporting + human review queue (admin, doc 11).
- **Reputation system:** helpful contributions earn reputation; low-quality/abusive behavior loses privileges.
- **No cheating aid:** during Live tests, discussion of live questions is locked until the window closes.
- **Privacy:** public profiles are opt-in; granular controls over what stats are visible.
- **Content quality loop:** highly-upvoted community solutions feed back into the PYQ library (after moderation), improving content for everyone.

### B.3 Metrics
- Doubt-resolution rate + time-to-first-answer, % questions with a community solution, group retention lift, report-resolution SLA.

## Score-impact justification
Gamification converts intention into daily consistency (the biggest real-world failure point) while rewarding score-relevant behavior. Community resolves doubts faster than solo study, surfaces better solutions/tricks, and adds accountability — all mapped to retention, weakness-fixing, and confidence. Vanity-only mechanics are explicitly excluded.
